Our Promise to You

Promise

To my fellow Yellow Jackets—

As an incoming student three years ago, I had no idea what to expect from Georgia Tech. In high school I applied to far too many colleges and submitted my Tech application without much thought. Stepping foot on campus for the first time at FASET, I made a conscious decision to make the most of my time here and find value in every experience. From skydiving with freshmen friends, to visiting Alaska through ORGT, to learning (the hard way) that I was not cut out for GT Rugby… I have grown to love Georgia Tech and the community that it represents.

As Student Body President, my mission will be to serve you and make your time as a student constructive and enriching. By focusing the efforts of SGA to achieve this central purpose, Brandie and I are confident we can positively impact campus for years to come.

My hope for next year is that every student at Tech will find as much significance in his or her experience as I have found in mine. If elected Student Body President, this will be my constant thought as I work to empower the student body to become the leaders and role models of tomorrow.

Yours in White and Gold,

Dillon Roseen

Fellow Ramblin’ Wrecks,

Little did I know when I stepped on campus in the Fall of 2010 how much the next four years would shape me. When I first came to Georgia Tech, I was very unsure of my college decision. However, with time I have grown to love this Institute and the people in it more than I would have thought possible. My time at Tech has been characterized by progress - growth as an engineer and as a person. I owe so much of who I am today to Georgia Tech.

If elected, my goal for the year is to empower students, both inside & outside SGA, to be leaders on campus, in the greater Atlanta community, and eventually in the world. Dillon and I plan to do this by streamlining the efforts of SGA into one central purpose: serving the student body and its needs.

My love for Georgia Tech and love for other students has lead me to run for the Vice President of the Student Government Association. I want to serve the students of Georgia Tech by pouring my last two semesters into making campus a better place. My desire is for all students to have the same incredible experience I have had the past four years.

In Progress & Service,
Brandie Banner

Goals

Georgia Tech student life is unlike any other college experience. We want students to learn a lot while they are here but also enjoy their time in college. We have pinpointed some areas that we want SGA to focus on in 2014-2015 so we can all love our time at Tech. The platform below is just the surface of what we have planned; download our full platform for specifics on how we will get this done!
Download the full platform here

Improving Student Life
Better Campus Infrastructure
Increasing Access to Technology
Revitalizing SGA

The quality of student life at Georgia Tech can be vastly improved through a few simple initiatives in our top priority areas. Improving student life means we can all have a positive and enriching experience during our time as students.

Academics

One of the largest areas of improvement we see is the relationship between students and faculty. We believe if these are improved, students will learn more because they will respect their professors more.

  • Increase one-to-one interaction between student and faculty
  • Address academic grievance policies' awareness and effectiveness
  • Improve GT 1000 to incorporate career readiness, traditions, and service elements
  • Restructure the academic calendar, drop deadlines and finals week
Sense of Community

Georgia Tech is a very diverse community yet it still has strong divisions within the student body. We would like to see stronger relationships built among the GT community and for that reason we are committed to building a more cohesive campus culture.

  • Foster inclusivity among populations at Tech, including International Students, LGTBTQIA, Greeks, Student Athletes, Transfer/Commuter Students
  • Promote class unity with enrollment class photos and service projects
School Spirit

Georgia Tech is respected worldwide for its reputation. However, we want students to be proud to go to Georgia Tech outside of academics. We want to bring school spirit back to Tech.

  • Focus more on Tech traditions as part of FASET, RATS Week, and GT 1000
  • Repurpose energy from "THWg" into a collaborative effort with UGA that promotes service, organizes events, and advocates student interests
Mental Health Resources

Georgia Tech is a tough place. We want to equip students with the resources necessary to succeed and to remind them how valuable they are as individuals in the Georgia Tech community.

  • Improve the Campus Counseling Center by improving the counselor-to-student ratio and creating more opportunities for student involvement
  • Implement the peer-to-peer counseling initiative
  • Follow through with recommendations outlined in the Mental Health White Paper
Library

The administration is planning big changes for the library, changing it from a book storage facility to a technology-focused study space. We want to make sure that student's interests are the focal point of the discussion in how best to repurpose this space.

  • Voice student input for the library's transformation into a multimedia and technology hub
  • Expand current library initiatives like the Highland Bakery Express and StartUp Lab
Student Center

Several groups around Georgia Tech are working to identify ways to expand the Student Center. We want to bring these groups together to formalize research, surveys, and recommendations on this issue.

  • Solicit student feedback on what features are needed in student centers
  • Support a multi-organizational task force to facilitate feedback and recommendations
  • Advocate to provide more space for student organizations

Georgia Tech was recently ranked #3 for use of technology among campuses. SGA should advocate and provide for more innovative technology resources.

JacketPages

SGA's website, JacketPages, has improved significantly this year. We want to continue those improvements by providing more resources via that site.

  • Utilize JacketPages to connect campus and organizations with SGA and other on-campus resources
  • Organize "how-to" posts and video tutorials to guide organizations through the funding resources
  • Enable block-style features to connect representatives and cabinet members with their constituents
Campus Resources

Georgia Tech and SGA should continue offering resources that improve academic and extracurricular campus experiences.

  • Develop a mobile application that centralizes information about campus events similar to the Emory Bubble
  • Expand CIOS and StartUp Initiatives
  • Offer and advertise more student-accessible software like Microsoft Office 365®

Have you or your friends ever wondered, “What does SGA even do?”. You are not alone. We understand that it is often difficult to see why SGA’s work matters and how it pertains to your day-to-day life as a student. Additionally, many inefficiencies exist within the current SGA structure that inhibit the impact we are able to have as an organization. We plan to make the work we do more transparent, effective, and easy to understand by implementing the following ideas.

External Communication

External, two-way communication will becoming a priority of our administration in order to more effectively achieve the mission of SGA by increasing its interaction with the Georgia Tech Community.

  • Improve polling and feedback mechanisms to assess and advocate student opinion for campus-wide issues
  • Advertise office hours in different locations and host coffee chats centered on various campus issues
Organizational Infrastructure

We will actively adjust specific areas of SGA’s composition to more effectively cater to the needs of students. By streamlining our organization, our job can be done more efficiently.

  • Provide online resources that address organizations' frequently asked questions about SGA funding, resources, etc.
  • Hold information sessions and tutorials for organizations seeking funding from SGA
  • Solicit feedback from organizations about the funding process and SGA overall
  • Allow more flexibility for groups to receive funding by establishing Joint Governing Boards
SGA Transparency

SGA's actions, role and resources will be better communicated to the student body via established campus platforms.

  • Emphasize SGA's off campus role in advocating student’s opinions on large-scale issues to the Georgia General Assembly and other external bodies
  • Post records of SGA business online in a timely manner
  • Update campus on legislative issues that pertain to college students
Internal Development and Revitalizatione

To increase communication and development among the Executive, Legislative, and Judicial Branches of Student Government. By increasing the capacity and cohesion of SGA and revitalizing the organization as a whole, we will more effectively be able to serve the student body.

  • Define the purpose of each role within SGA in a think-tank style meeting
  • Formalize the SGA transition process
  • Improve interaction among the three branches of SGA: Executive, Legislative and Judicial
  • Create leadership development initiatives within SGA and among campus leaders
